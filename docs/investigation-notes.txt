INVESTIGATION NOTES - Product Render Label Accuracy & Iterate Winners Archetype

## Current Architecture:
1. Product renders are PNG images uploaded by the user and stored in S3 via product_renders table
2. The iteration pipeline uses Gemini 3 Pro Image Preview (gemini-3-pro-image-preview) via nanoBananaPro.ts
3. The product render PNG is passed as inline_data to Gemini alongside a control image (the winning ad)
4. Gemini generates a new image that tries to composite the product into a new scene

## Label Accuracy Problem:
- Gemini receives the product render as a reference image but REGENERATES the entire image including the label
- AI image generation models cannot perfectly reproduce text on labels
- The "HYPERBURN" text, flavour text, and sub-text get approximated but not pixel-perfect

## Solution Options:
A. Post-processing compositing: Generate the background/scene with Gemini, then overlay the real product render on top using image compositing (sharp/canvas)
B. Inpainting approach: Generate the full image, then mask the product area and paste the real render
C. Prompt engineering: Add stronger instructions to preserve label text exactly (limited effectiveness)
D. Two-pass approach: Generate background only (no product), then composite product render on top

Option D (two-pass) is the most reliable:
1. Generate background scene WITHOUT the product (tell Gemini to leave a space)
2. Use sharp/canvas to composite the real product render PNG onto the generated background
3. This guarantees pixel-perfect labels since the real render is used
